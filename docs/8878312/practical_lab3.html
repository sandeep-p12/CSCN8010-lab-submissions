Takeaways from the fit:¶

  1. The slope represents the estimated change in disease progression for a change in BMI of one unit. When BMI is 0, the intercept depicts the disease progression (which may not be significant in this situation).

  2. The training and test losses (mean squared errors) show how well the model matches the data. A better match is indicated by lower values.

  3. You can compare the losses to a baseline (such as projecting the average rate of illness progression for all samples) to determine whether it is a good fit.
